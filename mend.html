Overview

Timeline: 10/2015 - 03/2016 (In Progress)
Project: Innovation in Action
Team size: 5
Type: App/UX design

Mend is a health app that aims to improves communication between patients with mental conditions and their therapist and health provider. Mend provides daily logs for the patient to record their mood, diary and a variety of measures to share with the therapist. The therapist can customize the content for the patient and utilize the data collected from the patient to better customize the therapy as well as discovering problems in an efficient manner.

UX Methods

Interview | Persona & Scenario | Wireframe | Heuristic Evaluation

Interview and Persona

We interviewed 4 professional psychologists and social workers (therapists) and 10 people who had diagnosed depression.
The interview questions for the therapists include
- details of the therapists' interaction with their patients,
- their biggest frustration during their work,
- what they think prevents the patient from getting better, and
- if there is a mobile app platform to assit them, what would they want it to be.
We asked the people with past mental conditions about their experience with condition and what they think would be helpful for them during their depressed periods.
We analyzed and prioritized the common concerns shared by the therapists and the patients and generated two personae for the therapist and the patient.


Storyboard

We used Bitstrips to create our storyboard based on the persona. We designed the flow for the interaction between the users and the app. It describes the scenario with visual illustrations for the clarity of the flow. The storyboards shows the context, the problem, users' pain point, fear, and motivation.
